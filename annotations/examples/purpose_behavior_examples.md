Example 1

Utterance:
“People eat carrot.”

Context:
Adult asked, “What do people eat?” during a picture-book activity.

Purpose:
PUR_INFORM

Evidence:
The utterance provides factual information directly responding to the adult’s question without requesting further action.

Example 2

Utterance:
“No, stop.”

Context:
Adult attempts to take away a toy to transition to another activity.

Purpose:
PUR_PROTEST

Evidence:
The utterance functions to reject and resist the proposed action in the immediate interaction.

Example 3

Utterance:
“Help me.”

Context:
Child is struggling to zip up a jacket before going outside.

Purpose:
PUR_REQUEST

Evidence:
The utterance aims to obtain assistance from another participant to complete an ongoing task.

Example 4

Utterance:
“Where scooter?”

Context:
Scooter is not visible after outdoor play; adult is nearby.

Purpose:
PUR_QUESTION

Evidence:
The utterance explicitly seeks information about the location of an object.

Example 5

Utterance:
“Look! Look!”

Context:
Child points at a passing bus while walking with an adult.

Purpose:
PUR_SOCIAL

Evidence:
The utterance functions to direct shared attention and maintain social engagement rather than convey new information.

Example 6

Utterance:
“I want go home.”

Context:
Child shows signs of overload near the end of a long shopping trip.

Purpose:
PUR_REGULATE

Evidence:
The utterance functions to reduce interaction demands and shift the situation toward a more stable state.
