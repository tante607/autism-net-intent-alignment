# Labeling Guide (v0.1)

This task uses **operational labels**.
Annotators should avoid psychological attributions.

## Purpose
Purpose = the functional goal inferred from immediate context and utterance.
Always provide short evidence grounded in context.

Examples:
- requesting (ask for an object/action)
- protesting (reject/avoid)
- informing (share information)
- social-connection (greet/engage)
- self-regulation (soothe/escape)



## Behavior
Behavior = what was observed (speech/gesture/motor/mixed).

## Alignment
- aligned: behavior plausibly supports the inferred purpose
- misaligned: behavior contradicts or fails to support the inferred purpose
- uncertain: not enough evidence or multiple plausible interpretations

## Purpose label set (v0.1)

- **PUR_REQUEST**  
  The speaker aims to obtain an object, action, assistance, or information from another participant.

- **PUR_PROTEST**  
  The speaker aims to reject, stop, avoid, or resist an ongoing or proposed action or situation.

- **PUR_INFORM**  
  The speaker aims to provide information, describe an observation, or report a state without explicitly requesting a response.

- **PUR_QUESTION**  
  The speaker aims to elicit specific information by posing an explicit question.

- **PUR_SOCIAL**  
  The speaker aims to initiate, maintain, or regulate social interaction (e.g., greeting, turn-taking, attention-directing).

- **PUR_REGULATE**  
  The speaker aims to manage interaction load or regain stability by reducing demands or shifting the interaction state.
